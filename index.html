Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

J.K. Rowling

Reserve

"Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" introduces us to the magical world of Hogwarts as Harry discovers his wizarding abilities. Alongside his friends Ron and Hermione, Harry faces challenges, unravels mysteries, and embraces his destiny as the Boy Who Lived. J.K. Rowling's enchanting tale is a timeless adventure of friendship and courage against the backdrop of a richly imagined wizarding universe.

The Great
Gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald

Reserve

"The Great Gatsby," F. Scott Fitzgerald's third book, is considered the pinnacle of his career. This iconic novel, set in the Jazz Age, tells the tale of the wealthy Jay Gatsby and his love for the beautiful Daisy Buchanan. Amidst lavish parties on Long Island during a time when "gin was the national drink and sex the national obsession," the story unfolds as an exquisitely crafted portrayal of America in the 1920s.

The Fault
in Our Stars

John Green

Reserve

Hazel, a terminal patient with a tumor-shrinking miracle, encounters Augustus Waters at a Cancer Support Group. In "The Fault in Our Stars" by John Green, their unexpected connection rewrites Hazel's story. This raw exploration of life and love stands as Green's most ambitious work, delivering insights into the tragic and thrilling aspects of existence.

Alice's Adventures in Wonderland

Lewis Carroll

Reserve

Lewis Carroll's iconic "Alice in Wonderland" conceals the lesser-known origin story of "Alice's Adventures Under Ground." Created as a gift for Alice Liddell, this manuscript, sold among collectors, now resides in the British Museum since 1948. In this edition, the 130-year-old manuscript is faithfully reproduced in color, offering a rare glimpse into Carroll's enduring world and the genesis of this timeless children's story.

It Ends with Us

Colleen Hoover


Reserve

Lily, a resilient woman from Maine, finds unexpected love with neurosurgeon Ryle Kincaid in Boston. Despite Ryle's assertiveness and aversion to relationships, Lily becomes the exception. However, questions about Ryle's past arise, and Lily's thoughts turn to her first love, Atlas Corrigan, endangering the life she's built. The complexities of love and past connections intertwine in this gripping tale.

Atomic Habits

James Clear


Reserve

"Atomic Habits" by James Clear provides a proven framework for daily improvement, offering practical strategies to form good habits and break bad ones. As a leading expert on habit formation, Clear transforms the way readers think about progress and success, providing essential tools for personal or collective goals, from quitting smoking to winning championships.

The Hunger Games

Suzanne Collins


Reserve

In the dystopian Panem, the Capitol governs through the annual Hunger Games, forcing each district to send a boy and girl for a televised fight to the death. Katniss Everdeen, a sixteen-year-old, volunteers to protect her sister, thrusting her into a deadly contest where survival demands choices that test her humanity and understanding of love.

The Art of War

Sun Tzu


Reserve

Sun Tzu's classic, "The Art of War," penned 2,500 years ago, remains a cornerstone of military strategy rooted in Chinese warfare principles. Embraced by diverse fields, including politics and business, its teachings transcend the battlefield. In today's context, the book serves as a guide for gaining advantages both in the boardroom and on the battlefield, offering timeless wisdom applicable to various aspects of life.

Jane Eyre

Charlotte Brontë


Reserve

Orphaned Jane Eyre, resilient and independent, becomes a governess at Thornfield Hall for the brooding Mr. Rochester. As their feelings grow, Jane discovers a dark secret, forcing a choice between staying with Rochester and facing consequences or following her convictions, potentially parting from her love. Brontë's intense and intriguing novel, "Jane Eyre," vividly depicts a woman's pursuit of equality and freedom.

Little Women

Louisa May Alcott


Reserve

Louisa May Alcott's classic, Little Women, follows the lives of the March sisters—talented Jo, fragile Beth, beautiful Meg, and romantic Amy—as they navigate challenges during the Civil War in New England. Based on Alcott's experiences, the novel explores timeless themes such as love, death, war, personal ambition, and cultural clashes between Europe and America, defying its initial perception as a mere "girl's book."

Pride & Prejudice

Jane Austen


Reserve

In Jane Austen's "Pride and Prejudice," Elizabeth Bennet learns the impact of quick judgments and the distinction between surface-level and genuine virtue. Mr. Bennet's financial challenges, with his estate's entailed nature, prompt the daughters to seek advantageous marriages for family support, driving the plot.

Les Misérables

Victor Hugo


Reserve

Victor Hugo's "Les Misérables" tells the story of Jean Valjean, an ex-convict striving for redemption. Despite his efforts to leave his criminal past, he faces challenges from his conscience and the relentless Inspector Javert. A case of mistaken identity complicates matters, but Valjean is determined to protect the daughter of Fantine, a woman driven to desperation by poverty.
